Mothers Out Front 1: Viewing People Power, DS 4200 S20

Rachel Waddell, Nanxi Gu, Maris McGuinness, Samyak Jain

Service-Learning Course Project as part of DS 4200: Information Presentation and Visualization, taught by Prof. Dylan Cashman, Data Visualization @ Khoury, Northeastern University.

Motivation

Currently, Mothers out Front, a nonprofit that enables people – with a focus on mothers – to pursue environmental justice is looking to improve their weekly newsletters to their base. They want to include visualization that allows people to see the current size of the movement in an engaging way, including the three steps of their "Ladder of Engagement:" Supporting (base who have not volunteered yet), Taking Action (volunteers), and Leading (people who take charge and organize). A secondary goal is to allow users to personalize the visualization by zooming in on their home state and even city, if a team exists there. To fulfill these needs, we have created a bar chart to show overall movement size stratified accross the three levels of involvement linked to a chloropleth map that allows zooming-in on states, which then filters the bar chart to the state data.
